Fish Tacos with Cabbage Slaw

Description

Tuesday nights have long been taco night in our house and one of our favorite recipes is this one with a spicy lime cabbage slaw. We've used this recipe with both white fish and shrimp and it's really slaw that makes the taco slap. As always, we recommend hot sauce and Louisiana is definitely the way to go.

Ingredients

For the fish

  • 4 filets white fish we usually use cod or tilapia
  • salt and pepper
  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tbsp canola oil

For the slaw

  • 4 cups chopped cabbage red or green or a combination of both
  • 1/4 cup chopped red onion
  • 3 tbsp distilled white vinegar
  • 1 tsp salt)
  • 2 tsp white sugar
  • 1/3 cup sour cream
  • 3 tbsp mayo
  • juice and zest of 1 lime
  • 2 tbsp minced fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 tsp kosher salt

For tacos

  • warmed tortillas
  • lime wedges
  • Louisiana hot sauce

Steps

  1. Pat the fish filets dry with paper towels and season with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. Combine paprika, cayenne, garlic powder,and cumin and rub mixture generously onto fish filets. Set aside while you prepare the slaw.
  3. Add all ingredients for slaw into a large bowl and mix well. Set aside to let those flavors marinate while you cook the fish.
  4. Add oil to a hot pan and sear fish filets over high heat for 4-5 minutes each side, or until cooked through.
  5. Assemble tacos, add a squeeze of lime, and don't forget that Louisiana hot sauce!
